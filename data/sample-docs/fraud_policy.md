# EuroFlow Payments Group fraud policy

EuroFlow operates transaction monitoring for wallets, cards, merchant acquiring and instant credit transfers. The fraud engine computes a risk score using device changes, new beneficiary age, transfer velocity, unusual amount, merchant category, country risk, prior account activity and known mule-account indicators.

High-risk transfers are held for manual review by the fraud operations team. Medium-risk transfers can require step-up authentication and a contextual customer warning. The fraud analyst can approve, reject or escalate a case.

The fraud operations team reviews alerts during extended business hours and tracks open alerts in the case management queue. Severe fraud patterns are escalated to the compliance lead, customer support lead and engineering incident channel with a severity label.

Rules are reviewed monthly using false positive rates, chargeback trends, support ticket feedback and confirmed fraud outcomes. The fraud owner maintains the transaction monitoring rules and documents rule changes in the control register.

EuroFlow participates in a voluntary fraud information sharing arrangement with partner PSPs. The arrangement defines permitted unique identifiers, retention limits, access controls and operational escalation contacts. A data protection impact assessment is maintained by Legal before new data categories are added.
